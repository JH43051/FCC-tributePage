Charles Martel

Father of the European Middle Ages

Notable events in Martel's life:


  • 688 AD. - Born to Pepin of Herstal (modern day Belgium) and wife Alpaida.
  • 714 AD. - Father Pepin dies, igniting a civil war for the throne in France.
  • 715 AD. - Charles escapes prison and is given the title "Mayor of the Palace" of Austrasia (eastern France).
  • 717 AD. - Invades the kingdom Neustria (central France) and is victorious at the battle of Vincy.
  • 718 AD. - Defeats the Saxons at the battle of the Teutoborg Forest; Charles spends several years subjugating various germanic tribes and consolidating his territory in France.
  • 721 AD. - The Emir of Cordoba (modern Spain) gathers a large north-African army and attacks Aquitaine (France).
  • 732 AD. - Martel's Frankish army defeats a vastly superior muslim army of Umayyads at the battle of Tours, saving France from further invasion.
  • 737 AD. - Is crowned "King of the Franks" (modern French).
  • 741 AD. - Dies.
