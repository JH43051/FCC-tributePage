J.R.R. Tolkien

The father of modern fantasy literature

Here's a timeline of Tolkien's life:


  • 1892 - Born in South Africa to Arthur and Mabel Tolkien.
  • 1902 - By the age of four, Tolkien could write fluently.
  • 1911 - Goes on a treck in Switzerland as a youth, inspiring the later journey of Bilbo in "The Hobbit".
  • 1913 - Tolkien, a Catholic, marries Edith Bratt, a Protestant, causing conflict with both families.
  • 1920 - Comes home to England from the first world war and becomes the youngest professor at the University of Leeds teaching English Language.
  • 1925 - Moves to Oxford as a fellow at Pembroke College after completing the definitive translation of the classic "Sir Gawain and the Green Knight".
  • 1926 - Translates the Anglo-Saxon tale "Beowulf".
  • 1926-35 - Completes several beloved "Middle Earth" works such as "The Hobbit" and the first two books of "The Lord of the Rings".
  • 1936-38 - Expands the mythology of "Middle Earth" (based largely on the pre-Christian germanic folk religion) through works such as "The Silmarillion".
  • 1939 - Serves in the second world war as a cyptography officer, or "code breaker".
  • 1945 - Finally finishes the "Lord of the Rings" trilogy by completing "The Return of the King", changing the course of fantasy literature forever.
  • 1959 - Retires and enjoys increasing literary fame up until his death.
  • 1971 - Dies at the age of 82.
